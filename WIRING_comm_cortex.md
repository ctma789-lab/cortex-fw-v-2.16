# Communication Cortex — Wiring & Pinout

**Firmware:** `comm_cortex_v2_1_6.ino` · **MCU:** ESP32-S3

> **VERIFY-BUILD sections** are marked below — the CD4053 failsafe/bypass path,
> level shifting, and power rails. Do **not** solder those from this document.
> They sit between the firmware and the propellers; build them only from your
> own bench-proven wiring. Everything else is taken directly from the firmware's
> pin definitions.

---

## 1. ESP32-S3 pin assignments (from firmware)

### Control / RC path
| Signal | GPIO | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| PIN_IBUS_RX | 4 | in | UART0 RX — iBUS from receiver (FS-X6B), 3.3 V direct |
| PIN_PPM_OUT | 6 | out | RMT PPM to CD4053 IN-B, 3.3 V, idle-HIGH |
| PIN_WD_PULSE | 5 | out | **50 Hz hardware watchdog pulse** → RC filter → CD4053 SELECT |

### Inter-cortex link (to Sensory Cortex) — UART1
| Signal | GPIO | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| PIN_SENSE_RX | 16 | in | SafetyPacket in from Sensory |
| PIN_SENSE_TX | 17 | out | ProximityCmd out to Sensory |

### Flight-controller telemetry passthrough — Serial2
| Signal | GPIO | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| PIN_TELEM_RX | 20 | in | Listen for receiver/TX sensor polls |
| PIN_TELEM_TX | 21 | out | Send sensor replies — **via 1 kΩ series resistor** |

### GPIO outputs
| Signal | GPIO | Channel | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| PIN_FLOOD | 1 | ch10 | Floodlight FET gate (HIGH = on) |
| PIN_VTX | 2 | ch11 | VTX power FET gate (HIGH = on) |
| PIN_LED | 48 | — | Onboard WS2812 RGB status LED |

### Navigation lights
| Signal | GPIO | Position | Colour |
|---|---|---|---|
| PIN_NAV_FL | 7 | Front-Left | Green |
| PIN_NAV_FR | 8 | Front-Right | Red |
| PIN_NAV_RL | 9 | Rear-Left | Green |
| PIN_NAV_RR | 10 | Rear-Right | Red |

## 2. iBUS input (from firmware)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Frame length | 32 bytes (2 header + 28 data + 2 CRC) |
| Header | 0x20 0x40 |
| Channels | 14 |
| Channel range | 1000–2000 µs (mid 1500, deadband 30) |

iBUS is 3.3 V logic and wires directly to GPIO4 — no level shifting on this line.

---

## 3. PPM output (from firmware)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Channels to Naza | 8 (ch1–7 + ch8 passthrough slot) |
| Frame period | 20 000 µs |
| Sync (LOW) pulse | 300 µs |
| RMT clock | 1 MHz (1 µs resolution) |

Generated by the ESP32-S3 **RMT** peripheral (hardware-timed, jitter-free), idle
HIGH, out on GPIO6 into the CD4053 IN-B.

## 5. Hardware failsafe — CD4053 bypass — **VERIFY-BUILD**

> **Do not build this from this document.** This is the last line of defence
> between firmware and props. The description below is functional only.

Functional intent:
- The CD4053 selects between the **CoRTX-modified** signal path (PPM from GPIO6,
  IN-B) and a **raw receiver** path (bypass), on its SELECT line.
- SELECT is driven by the Comm Cortex **watchdog pulse** (GPIO5, 50 Hz) through
  an **RC filter (≈47 kΩ + 1 µF)**. While the firmware runs, the pulse holds the
  4053 on the modified path.
- If the firmware stalls (task hang, crash, or TWDT panic-reset), the pulse
  stops, the RC filter discharges (~100–200 ms), and the 4053 reverts to the raw
  receiver path — the aircraft flies on its primary flight controller as if
  CoRTX were not present.

Verify on the bench: with props off, force a firmware hang (or hold the pulse
pin) and confirm the 4053 switches to raw PPM within the expected window, and
that the raw path actually reaches the Naza.

## 6. Level shifting & power — **VERIFY-BUILD**

> Intent from project notes, not a verified schematic. Confirm against your build.

- iBUS (GPIO4) and PPM (GPIO6) are 3.3 V logic; confirm the receiver/Naza logic
  levels your specific hardware presents on each line, and shift only where a
  5 V device requires it.
- Telemetry TX (GPIO21) uses a **1 kΩ series resistor** (already reflected in the
  firmware comments) on the shared telemetry line.
- Power tree (project intent): UBEC → 5 V rail → ESP VIN → on-board 3.3 V. Decouple
  each IC; keep the RMT/PPM and watchdog lines clean.

## 7. Boot-time serial output (USBSerial, 115200 baud)

```
[BOOT] Communication Cortex v2.1.6
[OK] TWDT armed: 2000ms panic-reset
...
```

The Comm Cortex boots with `sensorDead = true` (no valid sensory data assumed
until the first packet arrives — a deliberate safe-start), and restores avoidance
authority only after several consecutive good packets.

---

## 8. Bench telemetry

Set `static const bool DEBUG_SERIAL = true;` at the top of the config block,
recompile, open USBSerial at 115200. Streams the decision-side state — output
channels (R/P/T/Y/FM), avoidance scale, `rcLost`/`sensDead`/`RTH`/`land` flags,
active floor/brake engagement, and the blocked flags from Sensory — at ~10 Hz.
Set back to `false` for flight.

Watch on the bench:
- output channels stay within 1000–2000 µs (avoidance saturation);
- `sensDead` goes 1→0 only after link recovery;
- avoidance biases the expected axis when a target approaches a given sensor.
